    Greenhouse Gases

    Greenhouse gasses are compounds that trap heat in the atmosphere. The collection of these gasses in the atmosphere creates the greenhouse effect. Principal greenhouse gasses, carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide, are all created from fossil fuel use, deforestation, mass livestock farming, use of synthetic fertilizers, and industrial processes. How does deforestation create greenhouse gasses? When plants respire, they absorb carbon dioxide, when they are cut down that carbon dioxide is released into the air. Mass forest clearing causes mass carbon dioxide release. It is estimated that 15 percent of all greenhouse gas emissions come from deforestation, according to the WWF. Because of deforestation, not only are mass amounts of greenhouse gasses being released, plants that absorb greenhouse gasses are being killed.
